Resource Discussion

Although the necessary resources can be listed rather easily (a project manager, a filmmaker, and financing), actually procuring the latter two turns out to pose a chicken-and-egg type of problem, one that's due mainly to the large amounts of both time and money that would ultimately be consumed during the proposed film's production phase. The conundrum can be summarized in a single sentence: "Without financing, I won't be able to obtain the support of a filmmaker, but if I don't have the support of a filmmaker, I won't be able to obtain financing" ... while a more detailed description might be as follows:

On the one hand, without significant financing already in place, few if any filmmakers would be willing to participate -- they don't work for free, after all, so it's hard to imagine that they'd willingly incur a serious financial risk. (Moreover, most environmentally-oriented filmmakers appear to have little experience -- or interest -- in producing dramatic content, as most of them appear to work solely on documentaries and such.)

Conversely, without the active support of a filmmaker, it's hard to imagine that I could convince potential benefactors to provide financing -- i.e. that they'd accept my assertions as to the "makeability" of the film and the amount of money its production would consume ... or, for that matter, my assertion that I'm even trustworthy at all, given that my professional track record would seem both inaccessible and irrelevant.

(I should probably mention, by the way, that crowdfunding doesn't appear to offer a solution, as crowdfunding sites are designed in almost every case to support commercial products as opposed to altruistic endeavors ... and given furthermore that the amount of money needed would be immense, as would the bookkeeping effort if refunds were eventually required.)

The end result is that to get the project off the ground, I need to find either a filmmaker or a financial benefactor who's willing to take an initial leap of faith -- i.e. who's:

  1. willing to "get the ball rolling" by making an inital (and perhaps conditional) commitment of time or money, respectively, and who's
  2. willing to sing the project's praises in order to help me secure additional funding and/or talent.

You could lend a hand currently, therefore:

  • by alerting environmentally-conscious filmmakers (if you're acquainted with any) to the existence of this website ... or, if you happen to be an environmentally-conscious filmmaker yourself, by contacting me directly (at the e-mail address I've provided below), and/or
  • by alerting environmentally-conscious institutions, foundations or other organizations (if you're acquainted with any) to the existence of this website ... or, if you happen to be involved in such an organization yourself, by contacting me directly (at the e-mail address I've provided below).

As for finding a project manager, I suspect that the critical attributes would include a sense of excitement about the project and a gift for collaborating effectively with others, while extra points might be awarded if the contender has at least a nodding familiarity with filmmaking and/or non-profit management. Please note that the work would be unpaid pending the arrival of significant financing, at which point the manager could make his/her own decision regarding appropriate levels of compensation. Please note, furthermore, that as an unpaid consultant I'd be open to changes of almost any sort in the content, i.e. in the screenplay, the movie title, or elsewhere.

If you or someone else would like to explore the possibility of becoming project manager in more detail, please contact me at the e-mail address I've provided below.
